Support the PNSQC Community

For more than 40 years, PNSQC has brought together experts, practitioners, leaders, students, and innovators to share knowledge and advance the practice of software quality. We are a volunteer-run 501(c)(3) nonprofit. Everything we do is focused on education, community, and the advancement of quality.

Help Us Do More for Our Speakers

Most conferences ask speakers to pay their own way. PNSQC has been giving a $250 travel stipend to its speakers because we believe people who share their knowledge with the community shouldn't have to bear the full cost of doing so.

Our goal is to increase that support from $250 to $500 per speaker. That takes approximately $7,500 in additional funding.

If PNSQC has helped you learn, grow, build connections, or advance your career, please consider helping us support the people who make that possible.

Why It Matters

Speaking at a conference takes real commitment. Preparing a paper, building a presentation, traveling, and spending days away from work and family all cost something. The practitioners who share their experiences at PNSQC do it because they believe in helping others learn from real successes, failures, and lessons from the field.

In fact, several speakers in recent years have voluntarily declined their stipend and donated it back to PNSQC to support future presenters. That spirit of paying it forward is part of what makes this community what it is.

We have also held conference registration fees flat for five years because we believe cost shouldn't be the reason someone misses the opportunity to participate.

Investing in the Next Generation

Support for Students and Teachers

With the rapid emergence of technologies such as artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, and automation, we believe community education and knowledge sharing are more important than ever.

To help address this need, PNSQC is expanding its community outreach efforts through the launch of the PNSQC Innovation Day. The event brings students, educators, parents, professionals, and community members together to explore the evolving world of STEM through educational seminars, robotics demonstrations, and hands-on projects.

How Your Donation Helps

Your support helps PNSQC expand access to STEM education, technology awareness, and community engagement opportunities throughout the region.

Donations will help provide:

  • Outreach and engagement with underserved STEM programs and schools
  • Student ambassador and volunteer programs that connect schools and communities
  • Community AI awareness and education initiatives
  • Educational and outreach materials for students, teachers, and families

Most importantly, your donation helps make STEM and technology learning more accessible, approachable, engaging, and inclusive for students, educators, parents, and community members alike.
