Generative AI Policy

Guidelines for appropriate and ethical use of generative AI tools in conference submissions and participation.

As generative AI technologies become increasingly prevalent in professional and academic contexts, PNSQC has established clear guidelines for their appropriate use in conference submissions, presentations, and participation.

Our policy synthesizes best practices from leading academic institutions including Princeton University, Harvard University, Cornell University, and industry standards from organizations like Elsevier.

Guiding Principles

Transparency

Authors and presenters should disclose the use of generative AI tools in their work, including which tools were used and how they contributed to the final product.

Accountability

Submitters remain fully responsible for the accuracy, originality, and integrity of their work, regardless of AI assistance used in its creation.

Academic Integrity

Use of generative AI must not violate academic integrity standards, including proper attribution of ideas and avoidance of plagiarism.

Quality Standards

All submissions must meet PNSQC's quality standards for originality, relevance, and professional merit, with or without AI assistance.
