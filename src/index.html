Call for Abstracts — PNSQC 2026

Share your insights on software quality

For over 40 years, the Pacific Northwest Software Quality Conference has brought together practitioners and researchers to exchange practical lessons, engineering techniques, and new ideas that advance software quality.

Quality in the Age of Autonomy - PNSQC's 2026 Theme

As software increasingly incorporates automation, AI, and autonomous systems, ensuring quality requires new approaches to reliability, validation, and trust.

We invite practitioners, engineers, researchers, and leaders to submit abstracts and share their experience with the community.

Tracks include:

Quality Engineering & Systems Reliability
Organizational Quality & Leadership
Emerging Technologies and AI Systems
Tools & Productivity

AI-Augmented Manual Testing with PLUS QA

Upcoming event

Wednesday, Apr 22 · 6:00 PM to 8:00 PM PDT

Hybrid event · PLUS QA, 1725 SE Ash St · Portland, OR + online

RSVP:

Event Description

Join us for our April meetup, presented and hosted by PLUS QA in Southeast Portland!


This month, PLUS QA's VP Ryan Wilder and Lead Dev Gianni Zamora walk us through how they're building AI solutions that work within their well-practiced manual testing strategies.


It's a practical, human-centered take on AI in QA, expanding what manual testers can do while emphasizing the high-quality human expertise that great testing demands.

Fireside Chat with PNSQC Authors

Upcoming event

Thursday, Apr 2 · 5:30 PM to 7:30 PM PDT

Virtual event · Zoom

RSVP:

Event Description

If you have been considering submitting a paper to PNSQC but are not quite sure where to start, this session is for you.


Join your hosts Ying Ki Kwong and Gwendolyn Avington, along with other PNSQC authors, for a casual chat designed to demystify the PNSQC paper proposal process, especially for first-time or less-experienced authors.


We will walk through what makes a strong proposal, what reviewers are looking for, and how the process works from abstract to final paper.
