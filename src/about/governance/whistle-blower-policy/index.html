Whistle Blower Policy

Protection and procedures for reporting concerns about ethical, legal, or financial violations.

PNSQC is committed to maintaining the highest standards of ethical conduct and legal compliance. Our whistle blower policy encourages the reporting of suspected violations and protects those who report concerns in good faith.

This policy provides a mechanism for reporting and investigating allegations of wrongdoing while ensuring confidentiality and protection from retaliation.

Core Protections

No Retaliation

PNSQC prohibits retaliation against anyone who reports a concern in good faith, regardless of whether the allegation is ultimately substantiated.

Confidentiality

Reports are handled confidentially to the extent possible while still allowing for effective investigation and resolution.

Anonymous Reporting

Individuals may submit reports anonymously, though providing contact information may facilitate investigation and follow-up.

Good Faith Requirement

Reports should be made in good faith with reasonable belief that wrongdoing has occurred. Deliberately false or malicious reports are not protected.

What to Report

Examples of concerns that should be reported include:

  • Financial irregularities or suspected fraud
  • Violations of laws, regulations, or organizational policies
  • Conflicts of interest not properly disclosed
  • Misuse of organizational resources or assets
  • Ethical violations or actions harmful to the organization's mission
