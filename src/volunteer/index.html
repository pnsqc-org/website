About PNSQC

The Pacific Northwest Software Quality Conference is a volunteer-led nonprofit community for people who care about building better software. We host an annual conference and year-round programs where practitioners share papers, talks, workshops, and practical lessons from the software quality field.

How PNSQC Is Organized

PNSQC divides its work across committee-formed departments. Each group is led by a chair, who coordinates volunteers, keeps work moving, and communicates with other chairs so the organization stays aligned. Our board members oversee all our committees and pitch in where help is needed.

  • Community Outreach Builds relationships with schools, companies, and community groups to broaden who participates as attendees, speakers, authors, volunteers, and leaders.
  • Marketing Promotes content about PNSQC through a variety of different mediums, including email, social media, newsletters, blogs, videos, and podcasts.
  • Program Shapes conference and Meetup content through the oversight of our keynotes, invited speakers, technical papers and posters and their review process, workshops, and the tracks these all fit under.
  • Operations Keeps the organization's operations running smoothly with tools, technology, project boards, website administration, and internal support systems and processes.
  • Volunteer Management Recruits, welcomes, matches, trains, communicates with, and recognizes the volunteers who make PNSQC possible.
  • Sponsorships Builds sponsor and donor relationships, tracks outreach, coordinates commitments, and helps partners get value from supporting PNSQC.
  • Conference Management Coordinates venue details, materials, registration, food, room support, setup, teardown, and the on-site attendee experience.

Benefits of Volunteering for PNSQC

Volunteer incentives vary by type and level of contribution, but PNSQC offers a mix of practical event benefits, public recognition, career growth, and community access.

Conference Access

Volunteers can receive discounted or complimentary admission, early conference content access, volunteer lounge access, and reserved seating.

Professional Network

Meet software quality practitioners, speakers, sponsors, board members, and partner organizations through conference work, meetups, and volunteer-only gatherings.

Career Development

Build visible experience in event planning, program curation, outreach, marketing, sponsorships, project management, website operations, and nonprofit leadership.

Recognition

Active volunteers will be recognized through newsletter mentions, chair shout-outs, conference slides, proceedings acknowledgments, website call-outs, and special long-time contributor badge accents.

Mentorship and Leadership

Volunteers can receive mentorship from chairs and board members, request professional recommendations when appropriate, and grow toward chair or board-level leadership.

Appreciation and Support

PNSQC appreciates all the work our volunteers do with personalized thank-you notes, appreciation gifts, certificates or awards, expense reimbursements, and communication tools for active volunteers.

Example Volunteer Roles

These are not the only ways to help, but are some of the more popular ways to help PNSQC so you can see the range of work available.

Community Outreach

Outreach Specialist

Build relationships with companies, schools, and community groups that can connect more people to PNSQC. This role is a good fit if you like meeting new people and helping new communities find a place in software quality.

Marketing

Social Media Creator

Draft and publish content for professional and community platforms, promote meetups and conference news, and help volunteers, speakers, and attendees engage with PNSQC online.

Program

Technical Paper Reviewer

Review one to three papers during the conference preparation season and give authors thoughtful feedback on clarity, structure, and how well their ideas come through.

Operations

Website Developer

Help keep the PNSQC website current by turning approved updates into clean web content, improving existing pages, and translating practical ideas into a better visitor experience.

Volunteer Management

Volunteer Engagement Specialist

Welcome new volunteers, learn what they want to do, match them with the right chair or role, and help them understand time commitment, expectations, and next steps.

Sponsorships

Sponsor Relations Manager

Build relationships with companies, track outreach, coordinate commitments, and make sure sponsors have the details they need before and during the conference.

Conference Management

Attendee Experience Specialist

Be part of the in-person team that helps the conference feel smooth and welcoming by supporting check-in, attendee questions, food timing, social events, and same-day logistics.
