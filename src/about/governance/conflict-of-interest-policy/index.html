Conflict of Interest Policy

Ensuring transparency, accountability, and ethical decision-making in all PNSQC operations.

As a 501(c)3 non-profit organization, PNSQC is committed to maintaining the highest standards of transparency and ethical conduct. Our conflict of interest policy ensures that decisions are made in the best interest of the organization and the community we serve.

This policy applies to all board members, officers, volunteers, and individuals in positions of decision-making authority within PNSQC.

Core Principles

Full Disclosure

All potential conflicts of interest must be disclosed promptly and completely to the appropriate oversight body.

Recusal from Decisions

Individuals with conflicts of interest must abstain from voting or participating in decisions where the conflict exists.

Organizational Interest First

All decisions must prioritize the best interests of PNSQC and its mission over personal or external interests.

Documentation

All disclosures and recusals are documented in meeting minutes and organizational records.
